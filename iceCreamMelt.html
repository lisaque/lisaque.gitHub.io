Evaluating the likelihood of your Ice Cream being melted & how melted it is, based on the weather

In this algorithm, I created "rules" to determine how the data is processed. There are 3 states of "melted" that an Ice Cream can be: 1. "No Melt", 2. "Partially Melted", 3. "Fully Melted".

To increase accuracy, I made 15 rules for my algorithm, 5 for each state. Therefore when the weather details (temperature & wind-speed) are put in, determines which class the "melt" belongs in.

For example, on rule3 is: rule3 = ctrl.Rule(temperature['average'] & wind['poor'], melt['no melt'])

and rule13 is: rule13 = ctrl.Rule(temperature['average'] & wind['good'], melt['partially melted'])

Rules 1-5 contain "poor" wind (slow wind-speed) and differing weather states. Rules 11-15 contain "good" wind (high wind-speed) and differing weather states. This way the algorithm can determine multiple factors, in the evaluation of the degree of melted-ness of the Ice Cream.

Finally, there's an input section, to test the algorithm. User sets the temperature and wind-speed, then the results print, stating which class the "Melt" corresponds to most.
